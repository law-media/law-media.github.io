Trusts and Estate Planning

We understand the importance of a properly drafted will. Without a will, state law decides who will receive your property and who can administer your estate without consideration of your wishes or the needs of your family and friends. Your will guarantees that your assets are distributed according to your wishes and instructions.

Drafting involves various delicate concerns including custody, health-care issues, real estate, personal finances, and taxes. Our firm will ensure that your will is properly drafted, created and executed and will stand up in court.
