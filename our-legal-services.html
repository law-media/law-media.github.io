Trusts and Estate Planning

We understand the importance of a properly drafted will. Without a will, state law decides who will receive your property and who can administer your estate without consideration of your wishes or the needs of your family and friends. Your will guarantees that your assets are distributed according to your wishes and instructions.

Drafting involves various delicate concerns including custody, health-care issues, real estate, personal finances, and taxes. Our firm will ensure that your will is properly drafted, created and executed and will stand up in court.
In addition to will drafting, the attorneys at Lau-Kee Law Group are experienced in preparing other estate planning instruments to secure your family’s future. Our firm is determined to establish an estate plan that addresses your distinct needs. We can help minimize estate taxes, created directives in the event of incapacity, avoid probate in certain circumstances, protect your assets, provide for your children and loved ones and ensure that your requests and wishes are met.
Whether the client passed away with or without a Will or Trust, administrators, executors and trustees are required to handle a range of tasks with accountability to their heirs, the courts and the taxing authorities. Failing to do any of these timely or correctly can have a negative impact on the estate, estate assets, taxes and most of all its beneficiaries. At Lau-Kee Law Group, our attorneys helps administrators, executors, and trustees carry out their responsibilities, including:

    Admitting the Will to probate
    Petition to administer the estate
    Settling and distributing estate and/or trust assets

    Inventorying and collecting assets
    Accounting to beneficiaries
    Overseeing time sensitive issues, such as disposition of real and tangible property

What Are The Advantages Of A Prenup?

Key advantages of prenups include:

    Protecting property by defining which assets each spouse would be entitled to in a divorce
    Protecting one spouse from assuming the debt obligations of the other
    Preserving the inheritance rights of family members from previous marriages
    Setting limits on the amount of spousal support (alimony) one spouse would pay the other in a divorce
    Establishing the groundwork for resolving future disputes

A prenuptial agreement means little, however, if it does not hold up in court. And even when the agreement is valid, to serve your interests, it must be structured carefully, with close attention to your estate plan, as well as your personal goals and circumstances.

Skilled Guidance For Business Succession Planning

According to Forbes, only a third of family businesses successfully transition from the first generation to the second. Nonetheless, many people who have worked hard to build a business dream of that business continuing to thrive in the hands of their children and grandchildren. With skilled legal planning, it is possible.

At the Law-Media, Inc. in Manhattan, we work closely with business owners to guide them through the succession planning process. It is one part of the comprehensive estate planning services we provide to clients in New York City and throughout surrounding areas.
Keys To Success

What are the keys to the successful transfer of business assets from one generation to the next? Every situation is unique, but key considerations we take into account include:

    Choosing leadership: In many cases, certain heirs have taken a more active role in the business than others, making them a more natural fit for taking over ownership. You want to make sure the right people are involved, including business stakeholders within your family and key people outside the family who are integral to the business’s success.
    Reevaluating the business entity: At the formation stage of your business, you had to choose a business entity, such as limited liability company (LLC), an S-corporation or a C-corporation. We can help you determine if the business is better off in its current form or if changing its structure is in the best interests of the new ownership generation.
    Setting a timeline: Timing is crucial in ensuring that new management and ownership know their responsibilities going forward. It is often helpful for owners to work alongside their successors for some time, until the new leadership team is ready to take over the reins with confidence. Establishing a timeline with key milestones can aid in this process.
